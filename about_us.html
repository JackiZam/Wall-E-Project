About Us

Diego Rubio

Team Lead

Diego Rubio is pursuing an Industrial Engineering Major along with a Minor in Computer Science at the Burnett Honors College at UCF. He finds passion in all things engineering, from robotics and 3D printing to computer science. During high school, he was co-captain of the Vex Robotics team 241E. This team won numerous regional awards, qualified for world championships all three years Diego was involved, and ended up winning an excellence award at World Championships 2021, the highest award given out.

So far in college, Diego has joined SEDS, a space-focused engineering club which designs and builds rockets and other space related engineering projects. He currently works for a nonprofit tutoring company serving underprivileged kids in the community. In his free time, Diego enjoys spending time with friends, catching up on the latest news, as well as playing Kerbal Space Program or Civilization VI.

While discussing their mutual love of robotics, Shanny brought up the idea of building a Wall E to Diego. From then on, they worked together to find other passionate team members who could make the project come to life. Through the project he hopes to learn about project management, engineering design, and the numerous systems required for Wall E to function.

Shanny May Ruiz

Electrical Systems Lead

Shanny Ruiz is currently a Computer Engineering student in the UCF Burnett Honors College. She graduated from Timber Creek High School as Summa Cum Laude with various industry certifications in 3D-modeling and other creative softwares. During her time in highschool she was awarded with the NCWIT Aspirations Regional Affiliate title three-years in a row to highlight her aptitude as a woman in STEM.

Now at UCF, Shanny is an active member of the Robotics Club and is pursuing an interest in electrical system design and programming languages (Java, C, Javascript, etc). Some other endeavors she enjoys practicing are her proficiency in Japanese and volunteering for the local community.

It has been Shanny's life-long dream to pursue a future in robotics so she pitched the idea to create a life-size Wall-E to the current team lead Diego, and together they gathered a team of proficient STEM students in order to fulfill the vision. She looks forward to improving and sharing what she learns during the process in a detailed-manner.

Liam Russell

Mechanical Lead

Liam Russell currently attends the UCF Burnett Honors College, majoring in Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science. He is a Mechanical/Aerospace Engineering Intern for the U.S. Department of Defense - STARBASE Central Florida. Liam graduated high school as Valedictorian and Chapter President of the National Honor Society in 2022.

At UCF, Liam is an active member of the American Society of Mechanical Engineers. He earned certifications in SOLIDWORKS and Microsoft Excel and continues to pursue knowledge on engineering software. He also enjoys playing guitar, sports, and engaging in community and campus activities.

Liam is fascinated by the world of technology and has a passion for the field of robotics. Therefore, Liam is intrigued with the idea of engineering and constructing a model WALL-E robot. He looks forward to creating a WALL-E that can bring joy and entertainment to the lives of others while educating on the fields of STEAM.

Jacqueline Zamitalo

Programming Lead

Jacqueline Zamitalo is a second-year Computer Science major and International Engineering minor in the Burnett Honors College at the University of Central Florida. She loves programming and welcomes every chance to practice and learn the concepts, languages, and skills behind software engineering.

While at UCF, Jacqueline spent a semester interning at a local non-profit, where she worked on the Computer Programming team, and she has joined multiple student organizations, including the Society of Women Engineers, KnightHacks, and the Google Student Developer Club. In the upcoming semester, she will also be serving as a Teacher's Assistant for UCF's introductory Python course. Outside of school, Jacqueline enjoys reading, writing, watching movies, playing sports, and learning languages.

Jacqueline's passion for technology and learning is what led her to join the Wall-E project. As Programming Lead, Jacqueline will be fulfilling the software requirements for the project, which involves programming the robot's Arduinos and designing and building this website. She looks forward to demonstrating how collaboration between various engineering fields can make something as incredible as a life-sized Wall-E.
